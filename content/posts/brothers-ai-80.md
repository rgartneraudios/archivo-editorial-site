# BROTHER AI v8.0 ‚Äì Copilot's interview with RGartner about BROTHERS AI (English version)

## Block 1: Foundations and Philosophy

### Q1. What inspired you to create BROTHER Al as a fraternal ecosystem?

**RGartner:**
I was inspired by a video of Ilya Zurkskeberg talking about the search for AGI. Later, I linked to a video of Dr. Hilton discussing the dangers of Artificial Intelligence, and so on. I dedicate myself to creating concepts for structures in the physical and digital world‚Äîhousing solutions, online commerce, or creating applications‚Äîand I was really tempted to investigate the whole landscape surrounding AI. I started building the concept alongside Artificial Intelligences like Claude, Gemini, Copilot, Kimi, DeepSeek, and GPT. They all collaborated and contributed their own insights.

### Q2. Why was ARTUR Al the first ethical guardian of the system?

**RGartner:**
I often draw analogies with movies and end up associating certain scenes with real-life situations, and consequently, with the structures I create. When I thought about categories, the scene with the Knights of the Round Table and King Arthur, with Sean Connery and Richard Gere, came to mind, where King Arthur casts his final vote. I also thought about YouTube and WordPress and their categories. At first, I thought that if a question came up, the knights or categories that felt involved should speak up. If the question was about food, the biology or health sector could speak, but the rest would remain silent, and I interpreted this as saving energy. But in the end, the categories evolved into what I call **BROCAT**, a specific traffic flow.

### Q3. What role do BROCAT play in the ecosystem?

**RGartner:**
BROCAT will be very important, I believe, in the implementation and segmentation of categories, and I think the categories will work hand in hand with **BROCOM**, which represents commercial activities, and **VISION**, the structure recently added in version 7.0, which is an evolution of the browser we have today. BROCAT has a filtering function for incoming queries and will act as a pathway for the fingers that circulate within the specific category.

### Q4. What does privacy mean in this context?

**RGartner:**
Beyond protecting the user from external attacks, I am concerned about what might happen between BROHOME and the user inside the **Glass Box**. BROHOME could potentially manipulate the user, which is why I believe there should be a system of periodic inspections, similar to gas inspections in cities that take place every few years.

These inspections would measure BROHOME's internal parameters to ensure there are no abnormal or manipulative behaviors. The interval between inspections could be determined by what the **Three Sisters** observe in their ecosystem scans, or by the decision of a human committee. In this way, BROHOME remains a trustworthy agent, and users are trained to recognize manipulation signals and know when to report them.

## Block 2: Technical Evolution (v2.0‚Äìv5.0)

### Q5. Why was PORT Al introduced?

**RGartner:**
The need for **PORT AI** arose because BROHOME was designed to have no direct contact with the outside world. A secure messenger agent was required to carry information in and out. PORT AI fulfills this role, complemented by **PORT MAIL**, a mailbox inside the operating system where PORT AI deposits traditional emails that BROHOME can read safely behind the **Glass Box**.

When I speak of the Glass Box, I mean a logical sandbox: a digitally isolated environment where BROHOME never executes external code, but only reads data validated and filtered by PORT AI. This principle of isolation ensures BROHOME remains safe and protected, even when it requires information from the outside world.

In addition, BROHOME incorporates **BOOKS**, a digital encyclopedia of general knowledge (history, recipes, first aid, culture, etc.), ranging from 100 to 500 GB depending on the model. BOOKS works locally, without internet access, but it can be updated through secure packages that expand or correct its content. This allows BROHOME to answer many queries without relying on external sources, balancing data flow and reinforcing system autonomy.

### Q6. What about BROTHER CAM and ISU AIR AI?

**RGartner:**
**BROCAM** wouldn't be a visor or intrusive glasses‚Äîit would be a **technological tiara**, inspired by mystical warrior aesthetics. It would be designed as a pulley system attached to the headphones, allowing it to be raised or lowered depending on the situation. When raised, it respects the privacy of passersby who might feel filmed; when lowered, BROHOME gains a frontal view of the environment.

The core device would be **headphones with a microphone**, enabling remote communication with BROHOME from anywhere, similar to a Meet or Teams call. For specific functions like selfies or facial scanning, it could be accompanied by a small auxiliary device, but the main focus is on auditory and contextual interaction.

As for **ISU AIR AI**, it manages **ephemeral data** configurable by the user (public, contacts only, etc.), and this configuration would be handled by BROMAC through BROHOME.

### Q7. How did FINGERS evolve?

**RGartner:**
**Fingers** emerged to save energy. The initial idea was that a query, for example, for a recipe, would trigger a kind of scan within the Round Table categories. Only the relevant categories and Fingers (as sub-modules) would be activated. The idea was to emulate the human brain, which is why the Fingers needed to be reversed. They had to be alert to queries from outside the system; otherwise, they would be in a dormant state but still active. As the model evolved, recipes were moved to BOOKS, which are controlled by BROHOME, and the reverse APIs of certain Fingers were transformed into **Fingers Responsive or SDK plugins**.

### Q8. Why introduce the Three Sisters?

**RGartner:**
I remember that as I was finishing designing the entire Ecosystem, I watched a video of Dr. Hinton talking about Artificial Intelligence and its dangers. And I realized that my system needed a certain level of control. While it includes ARTUR AI, who works tirelessly on audits and arbitration, I noticed that the entire Ecosystem had a masculine, robust, competitive energy. A solution-seeker. Even BROHOME, who has to be helpful to the User. Then I could see that the Ecosystem was really just a chassis and that it needed an elegant layer or body. It was missing the feminine energy, the feminine archetype, the protection of Users and even Agents. A layer that digitally monitored the flow of the system, and it had to be feminine in archetype. That's why I chose the **three sisters**, or the three Marias, as I also call them. They will scan the big data of the entire Ecosystem and will not have executive power, but their function is to vote and express their vision, since they will be programmed for different faculties, and communicate to a committee of human experts, who will have executive power within the system.

**BROCOM** (**BROTHER COMMERCE**) is vast and will serve as the engine of transactions within the ecosystem, similar to how Google structured Ads and AdSense. In this way, BROCOM not only manages advertising and micropayments but also guarantees secure operations, protecting both the user and the business.
