English#

The Paradox of Infinite Knowledge#

We live in the most extraordinary era in human history for learning. Never before has all the world’s information been so accessible, so fast, so available. And yet, the online course industry bills billions a year selling packaged knowledge.

The irony? Most of that knowledge is available for free. The difference is not the content. It’s knowing HOW to access it, structure it, and apply it.

And that’s where AI changes the whole game.

The Method No One Teaches You: Chatting with AI as a Tutor#

Most people use AI like a glorified search engine: they ask a question, get an answer, copy and paste. That’s wasting 90% of its potential.

AI is not Google. It’s a personalized tutor available 24/7 that adapts to your level, your context, and your specific needs.

Step 1: Level Diagnosis#

Before asking “how do I do X,” give it context to evaluate where you are:

Example - Learning sales from scratch:

“I want to learn sales techniques. I’ve never professionally closed a sale, but I need to start selling [your product/service].

Ask me 10 questions to assess my current knowledge level in sales and recommend a personalized 30-day learning plan.”

The AI will ask you questions like:

  • Have you interacted with customers before?
  • Do you know the difference between prospecting and closing?
  • What kind of objections are you afraid to face?
  • Is your product B2B or B2C?

Based on your answers, it automatically adjusts the level. It won’t talk to you about “advanced conversion funnels” if you don’t know what an objection is.

Step 2: Context Building (the key everyone ignores)#

Don’t ask the AI isolated questions. Give it the full picture:

Wrong: “How do I learn English?”

Right:

“I’m 30 years old, a native Spanish speaker, with an A2 level of English (I can read basic texts but not hold conversations). I need conversational English for work meetings in 6 months. I can dedicate 30 minutes daily.

Design a realistic plan with practical exercises I can do on my own.”

The difference is abysmal. The first question gives you generic answers. The second gives you a personalized program with specific exercises, measurable goals, and tailored to your real life.

Step 3: Structured Progression with Measurable Milestones#

Ask the AI to divide the learning into clear stages with checkpoints:

“From the plan you gave me, I want you to divide it into 4 monthly milestones. For each milestone, give me:

  1. What I should be able to do upon completion
  2. 3 practical exercises to verify that I achieved it
  3. Signs that I’m ready for the next level”

This turns abstract learning into concrete, measurable progress.

Real Case: How I Created My Blog Without Knowing How to Code#

This is my personal and fully verifiable case.

I needed a blog. Traditional options:

  • Hire a programmer: $500-$2,000+
  • Take a programming course: months of study
  • No-code platforms: $10-$30/month with limitations

What I did:

  1. I started with Copilot (GitHub’s AI): I explained that I wanted to create a static and economical blog, but I didn’t know where to start. Copilot suggested I use GitHub Pages, a platform I was completely unaware of. Not only that, it introduced me to Hugo, a static site generator, and helped me with the initial structure.
  2. I learned new tools in the process: Copilot taught me how to use PowerShell to upload the blog to GitHub. Before this, PowerShell was a total mystery to me. Now I use it regularly to update the site.
  3. Copilot got stuck on certain technical details of configuration and customization (it happens, AIs have limits).
  4. I continued with Claude: I explained exactly where I was, what I had tried with Copilot, what worked, and what didn’t. Claude helped me solve the Hugo configuration problems and customize the design.
  5. Result: A functional blog with Hugo, deployed on GitHub Pages, updated via PowerShell, personalized, with no hosting costs, and I also learned three new tools in the process (Hugo, PowerShell, Git).

Total cost: $0 if you use free versions, or ~$20/month for Claude Pro if you want more capacity.

Time: A few days working on and off.

The most important thing: I didn’t just copy and paste code. I understand how it works because the AI explained each step. Now I can maintain and modify it myself.

The Double Game: Paying for Courses to Learn How to Use a Free Tool#

My experience with the blog demonstrates the immense benefit of learning with AI: it’s accessible, personalized, and practically free. However, a great paradox has emerged: while AI offers us this power, an industry of courses that promise to “teach you how to use AI” for hundreds or thousands of dollars is flourishing.

This is like selling you a map to a treasure you already have in your pocket. What they teach you in these courses, for the most part, you can learn simply by talking to the AI itself. This leads us to a key question: if the knowledge is there, why do people pay? Because the sale of these courses is often not based on a unique educational value, but on something much older and more manipulative.

The Opaque Tactics of the Course Industry#

Now let’s talk about why so many people keep paying for knowledge that is available for free.

It’s not because the content is better. It’s because of psychologically designed sales techniques to get you to say yes.

The “Shills” or Plants: The Dirtiest Tactic#

In expensive face-to-face training events (workshops, intensive seminars), there is a known but little-discussed practice: the plants or “shills.”

These are people paid by the organization, mixed in with the real attendees, whose job is:

  1. Create artificial momentum: “I already signed up, this is incredible”
  2. Normalize exorbitant expenses: If they see others paying without hesitation, it seems reasonable.
  3. Generate intense FOMO: “30% of the room has already reserved their spot”
  4. Isolate those who doubt: Make you feel like YOU are the weird one for questioning.

How to spot plants?

  • There is always a magic percentage that “already bought” (typically 25-35%).
  • “Coincidentally” a line forms to sign up as soon as the sales pitch ends.
  • The testimonials sound too perfect at the right moment.
  • No one asks uncomfortable questions; everyone nods and applauds in sync.

This is not a conspiracy. It is documented sales engineering since the 80s in the high-ticket seminar industry. It is taught in sales manuals, and there are consultants specialized in recruiting and training shills.

AI never uses these tactics. There are no “last chance” timers, no “only 3 spots left,” no emotional pressure. It gives you information, period. You decide what to do with it.

Other Common Tactics#

Artificial Scarcity:

  • “This price is only available today”
  • “We only accept 20 people” (but they say it every month)
  • Countdown timers that magically reset.

Access to the “guru”:

  • They sell you access to the expert.
  • In reality, it’s their team who gives the classes.
  • The guru appears for 1 hour for a motivational talk.
  • And their talk is recycled content from YouTube.

“Exclusive” content that is not:

  • They call it a “unique methodology.”
  • It’s standard industry information with another name.
  • The promise is “how it’s structured.”
  • But the AI can create that structure for you in minutes.

AI in Your Daily Life: Beyond Great Skills#

The usefulness of AI is not limited to learning complex skills. It extends to solving small daily problems that consume our time and energy. Think of it as a personal assistant.

For example, you don’t know what to cook and have random ingredients in the fridge? Instead of looking for generic recipes, you can ask it: “I have two tomatoes, half an onion, some cheese, and pasta. Give me a quick recipe for dinner.” In seconds, it creates a delicious and practical plan.

Or maybe you need to write an important email in a professional tone, plan a weekend trip itinerary on a limited budget, or simply understand a complex concept you read in the news.

But it goes beyond the simple. AI also helps you investigate dense problems from multiple angles. Imagine your YouTube videos are not getting views. You ask the AI why it might be happening. It gives you an answer. But if it doesn’t convince you or you feel there’s more to it, you can ask: “Give me three other possible causes you haven’t mentioned” or “Analyze it from the point of view of the YouTube algorithm, then from the quality of the content, and finally from the SEO strategy.”

Thus, AI helps you see the much broader picture, exploring avenues of research that you might not have considered on your own. It doesn’t give you ONE closed answer. It opens up the full range of possibilities for you to choose where to investigate further.

AI is that multipurpose tool that helps you get ahead in a myriad of situations. I believe it’s all about stimulating the AI by giving it context and then flowing with it.

Topics Where AI Saves You a Fortune#

Digital Marketing#

You don’t need a $1,500 course. Ask the AI:

“I have a business in [X]. My audience is [Y]. Marketing budget: [Z]. Design a content strategy for the next 3 months with metrics to measure success.”

Languages#

You don’t need Duolingo Premium or expensive classes:

“I’m [level]. I want to practice conversation about [topics of your interest]. Speak to me only in English, correct my mistakes explaining why, and adjust the difficulty according to my performance.”

Programming#

Like my blog case. Give it context, go step by step, ask when you get stuck.

Personal Finance#

“I earn [X] monthly, my fixed expenses are [Y], I want to save for [Z]. Make me a realistic budget and teach me basic investment concepts appropriate for my risk profile.”

Sales#

We saw it above. AI can roleplay as a client, give you objections, evaluate your pitch.

Graphic Design#

“I need a [type of design] for [purpose]. My audience is [X]. Give me 5 different concepts with recommended color palettes, typographies, and the reason why each one works for my case.”

Then use Canva (free) or GIMP (free) to execute it.

When It Is Worth Paying for Education#

To not sound extremist: there are cases where paying makes sense.

Necessary Official Certifications:#

  • Medicine, law, civil engineering, etc.
  • They require formal degrees by regulation.
  • AI can help you study, but it does not replace accreditation.

1-on-1 Mentoring with Measurable Results:#

  • Someone who has achieved exactly what you want.
  • With documented and verifiable cases.
  • Who gives you personalized feedback on YOUR specific work.
  • With guarantees or clear conditions for success.

Access to Specific Networks/Communities:#

  • Sometimes you pay to be in the room with certain people.
  • This can have real value if those connections open doors.
  • But ask yourself: do I really need to pay to meet people in my industry? (LinkedIn, local events, free online communities).

Specialized Tools/Software:#

  • Access to platforms that cost money to maintain.
  • Proprietary data or tools that do not exist for free.
  • Here it can make sense if it is the only way to access them.

But always ask yourself:

  1. Can I get 80% of the value for free with AI?
  2. Does the remaining 20% justify the price they are asking?
  3. Are there measurable guarantees of results?
  4. Can I talk to people who paid and are NOT in their official testimonials?

Willpower is the Only Thing You Can’t Outsource#

You have the encyclopedia of the world in your pocket. The sum of human knowledge available 24/7. The difference between who learns and who doesn’t is no longer access.

It’s the willpower to:

  • Ask the right questions.
  • Provide sufficient context.
  • Practice what you learn.
  • Iterate when something doesn’t work.
  • Keep going when you get stuck.

The gurus sell you:

  • Structure ‚Üí The AI gives it to you personalized.
  • Community ‚Üí Reddit, Discord, specialized forums (free).
  • Access to the expert ‚Üí The AI doesn’t sleep, doesn’t charge extra per question, doesn’t have a bad day.
  • Motivation ‚Üí This one you have to bring yourself (and it’s the only one that really matters).

AI is not going to do the work for you. It’s not going to force you to practice. It’s not going to make you get up at 6 AM to study.

But if YOU bring the willpower, the AI gives you:

  • A tutor that adapts to your level.
  • Unlimited explanations until you understand.
  • Infinite practice (roleplays, exercises, corrections).
  • Zero judgment when you make a mistake.
  • And all this for a fraction of what courses cost.

Conclusion: The Future of Learning is Already Here#

The question is not “Where do I find the best course?”

The question is “Do I have the will to learn?”

If the answer is yes, you already have everything you need.

The free university is in your pocket. You just have to learn how to talk to it.
